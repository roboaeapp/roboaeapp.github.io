💪 Wilks Score Calculator

The Wilks Score is a coefficient used in powerlifting to compare lifters of different body weights. Enter your body weight, total, and sex to calculate your Wilks Score.

Formula: Wilks = Total × (500 ÷ (a + b·BW + c·BW² + d·BW³ + e·BW⁴ + f·BW⁵))  —  coefficients differ by sex. A score of 400+ is competitive nationally.

🏆 IPF GL Points Calculator

IPF GL (Good Lift) Points are the official scoring system used at IPF World Championships. They provide a more accurate cross-bodyweight comparison than the original Wilks formula.

Formula: GL Points = 100 × Total ÷ (a × BW0.75 − b × BW0.25 − c)  —  with separate coefficients for each sex. 500 GL Points is an elite benchmark.

🎯 Meet Attempt Selector

Enter your training maxes (best lifts in a training block) and this tool will recommend opening attempts, second attempts, and competition PR thirds for your next meet. Based on industry-standard attempt selection percentages.

Strategy: Opener = a weight you could triple on a bad day (~90–92% of max). Second = conservative PR territory (~97–100%). Third = stretch PR or competition record (>100%).
Please enter valid values for at least one lift.
Note: These are recommendations only. Adjust based on your warm-up performance, competition conditions, and coaching guidance. Always prioritize the opener — a successful opener builds momentum for the entire session.
