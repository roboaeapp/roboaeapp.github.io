Attempt Selection Guidelines

  1. Opener — a weight you could triple on a bad day (roughly 90–92% of max)
  2. Second attempt — conservative PR territory (96–100%)
  3. Third attempt — competition PR or stretch goal (>100%)

Technique Notes

Squat Cues

Maintain chest up and elbows forward throughout the descent. Drive the knees out against the belt of tension created by the hip flexors. Think "spread the floor" on the way up.

Bench Press Cues

Set the arch before unracking. Squeeze the bar hard to activate the lats. Touch low sternum, not the collarbone. Drive through the legs to transfer leg drive into the press.

Deadlift Cues

Push the floor away — don't think of it as a pull.
Keep the bar dragging the shins. Lock hips through at the top.

Nutrition & Weight Class

Competing in the 93 kg (205 lb) class. Water cut is minimal — primary strategy is to walk around at competition weight without a significant cut. Caloric intake is managed during the off-season to allow slow, lean mass gain without excessive fat accumulation.

Pre-Meet Week Checklist

  1. Drop sodium 3 days out to reduce water retention
  2. Scale down carbohydrates slightly 2 days out
  3. Carb load the night before with familiar foods
  4. Weigh in — confirm weight class eligibility
  5. Refeed immediately after weigh-in (2–3 hours to competition)
  6. Warm up systematically — hit opener depth in the back room
