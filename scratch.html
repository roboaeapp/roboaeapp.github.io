The Big Three Lifts

Why These Movements?

The squat, bench press, and deadlift are the three competition lifts in powerlifting. Together they test total lower-body strength, upper-body pressing power, and posterior chain dominance.

Competition Standards

  1. Squat — break parallel, command rack, no hitching
    • Break hip crease below top of knee (depth standard)
    • Wait for the "Squat" and "Rack" commands
    • Knees out, chest up, drive through the floor
  2. Bench Press — pause on chest, press on command
    • Full pause — bar must visibly stop on chest
    • Feet flat or on a platform (federation-dependent)
    • No butt rise; both glutes must remain on the bench
  3. Deadlift — lock out hips and knees, controlled descent
    • Bar must be lifted from a dead stop on the floor
    • Hips and knees fully locked at the top
    • Bar must be lowered in a controlled manner

Meet Day Order of Events

  1. Weigh-in and rack heights submission
  2. Warm-up period (typically 2 hours)
  3. Squat — 3 attempts per lifter
  4. Bench Press — 3 attempts per lifter
  5. Deadlift — 3 attempts per lifter
  6. Awards and total calculation

Sample Training Programs

Beginner Linear Progression

New lifters respond best to simple, frequent stimulus. Add weight every session and keep technique the priority. The goal is not maximal weight — it is maximal movement quality under load.

Weekly Template

  • Monday — Squat, Bench, Deadlift
  • Wednesday — Squat, Overhead Press, Deadlift variation
  • Friday — Squat, Bench, Deadlift
